Arabica coffee originated in Ethiopia. Ethiopia is Africa's biggest producer of coffee, and the world's sixth largest producer. Ethiopians are among Africa's main coffee consumers and the increase in Ethiopia's coffee consumption is increasing gradually. Near to 50 percent of coffee supply in the world is consumed domestically. coffee is definitely the backbone of Ethiopia's economy industry it takes up about more than half of the foreign economy exchange. in Ethiopia the very well-known coffee type grows in the forest area of the southwestern highlands of the Kaffa and Buno districts, which the coffee made is said to be organic. Another key major point in making the coffee industry successful is by exporting the coffee in green coffee beans form for the world, giving Ethiopia a boost in the international market. socially I think it's actually a great idea, since Coffee is becoming one of the essentials in daily human life's, who wouldn't want to get the best type of coffee to get their morning awakening with fresh smelling coffee, and also Ethiopians tend to do everything together, celebrate together, grieve together, and like to grow together, this would be an amazing thing to work on for the people. As stated above since coffee originated in Ethiopia there is a different way to make coffee which is called "coffee ceremony" that take up to 30–45 minutes, this whole process is a considered as one of the culture, which people usually use this time to gather around and enjoy their social life.

Ethiopia has one of the richest histories in Africa. It is mentioned in the Bible, and the prophet Mohammad fled into exile there. The beauty of Ethiopian women has been legendary since the times of King Solomon when the Queen of Sheba visited him in search of wisdom. According to Ethiopian legend, the Queen later gave birth to a son, Ibn-al-Malik (known as Menelik), the founder of the Ethiopian Dynasty. The country was never colonised by a European power, but was occupied by the Italians in 1936. There are remains of Italian influence in the country: The magnificent bathtubs, the jacuzzis, and the furniture in the hotels. Athletics is synonymous with Ethiopia; locals revere their own athletes as well as those from other countries. The language of the Amharas, Amharic, has been written for more than 3,000 years; today it is the official language, taught in schools. Newspapers, radio and the only TV station all use Amharic as the language of communication. They also learn English, but only at Advanced Level and the universities. There are two major religions — the majority Christians and the minority Muslims. Among Christians, there are the Orthodox, Catholic and Protestant denominations. I admired the Orthodox followers, the men and women dressed in white flowing robes; the women also had their heads covered.
